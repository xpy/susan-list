Paper and size specifications

The paper I use is Bristol Smooth and I always leave a margin around the drawing.

A frame is not included so you can frame it yourself and use a frame of your choice.

How to get your own photorealistic portrait

There are four simple steps on getting your own photorealistic portrait

  1. Contact me with via e-mail and include up to three candidate pictures that you would like to be drawn.
  2. Together, we chose one of the pictures, decide the size, estimate a delivery date, and agree on a price.
  3. Your portrait arrives at your address around the agreed delivery date.

Choosing a photo

Any decent quality photo is fine, but the size and quality of the portrait is really dependant on the size and quality of the photo. Ideally it should be a close up portrait photo. Picking/cropping a face from a distant photo could also work but usually the result cannot be bigger than A4.

The best result is of course if you take your time and take a photo especially for the portrait.

How long does it take?

Depending on the size and complexity of the picture, it can be between one to five weeks. Once I see the image, I can be very accurate on the time estimation so you don't have to worry about this.

If you are planning to gift a portrait for a birthday, an anniversary or any other festive event that has a fixed date, I would suggest that you contact me at least six weeks earlier to make sure that we can make it in time.

For bigger events such as Christmas, I would suggest that you contact me at least two months earlier, since I usually have more commissions and we have to plan accordingly.

Delivery methods.

I usually send the portraits via post, but if you prefer a courier service, that is also possible. If you live in the Netherlands, we can also arrange to meet so I can give it to you personally.
